Nachos

Description

Nachos are a popular snack or appetizer originating from Mexican cuisine. They typically consist of crispy tortilla chips that are topped with a variety of ingredients, often including melted cheese, jalapeño peppers, sour cream, guacamole, and salsa. Nachos can be customized with additional toppings such as seasoned ground beef, shredded chicken, or beans, making them a versatile and customizable dish. They are known for their savory and often spicy flavors and are commonly enjoyed at sporting events, movie theaters, and as a casual snack or party food.

Ingredients

  • Tortilla chips
  • Shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a blend)
  • Ground beef or shredded chicken (optional)
  • Salsa
  • Sour cream
  • Guacamole
  • Jalapeño slices (optional)
  • Black olives (optional)
  • Chopped green onions (optional)
  • Refried beans (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Spread a layer of tortilla chips evenly on a baking sheet or oven-safe dish.
  3. If using ground beef or shredded chicken, cook it in a skillet over medium heat until fully cooked and seasoned with taco seasoning.
  4. Sprinkle a generous amount of shredded cheese over the tortilla chips.
  5. If you have refried beans, warm them up and spoon them over the cheese.
  6. Add the cooked meat (if using) evenly on top of the chips.
  7. Place the baking sheet or dish in the preheated oven and bake for about 10-15 minutes or until the cheese is fully melted and bubbly.
  8. Carefully remove the nachos from the oven.
  9. Top the nachos with salsa, dollops of sour cream, guacamole, jalapeño slices, black olives, and chopped green onions as desired.
  10. Serve the nachos hot and enjoy!
